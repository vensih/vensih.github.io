About the project

A professional website built from scratch for a notary office in Shkodër, Albania with over 25 years of practice. The brief was a clean, trustworthy presence that works for everyone: locals in Albanian, expats and international clients in English, Italian, and German. Everything from the CSS architecture to the SEO structured data was written by hand, no frameworks, no CMS. The services fee table, language routing, animated interactions, and mobile nav drawer were all custom-built to fit the client's workflow and the expectations of a legal services audience.

What was built

Every detail considered

Four Languages

Albanian, English, Italian, and German, each a separate set of pages with its own URLs and translated copy. Preferred language is remembered for returning visitors.

SEO Architecture

Full search and social metadata written by hand for every page in every language. No plugin, no generator. Every language version is indexed and discoverable independently.

Live Office Status

The contact page checks Tirana time on load and shows whether the office is currently open. Hours switch automatically between summer and standard schedules every year.

Clipboard Interactions

On desktop, clicking the phone or email copies it to the clipboard. A confirmation tooltip appears in whichever language the visitor is using at the time.

Slime Language Indicator

The active language pill has a sliding indicator where the leading edge snaps ahead and the trailing edge springs behind, creating a stretching effect as you switch languages.

Dynamic Years Counter

The years of experience shown on the page are calculated live from the founding year 1998. The number increments every January automatically, never needing a manual edit.

Challenges

What made it interesting

Making legal content readable

A notary's fee schedule is not naturally engaging material. The challenge was presenting dozens of rows of tariff data in a way that felt organized and scannable without stripping out the detail clients actually need. Collapsible tables with smooth animation kept the page from feeling like a wall of text.

16 pages of metadata, done right

Every language version needed its own complete set of titles, descriptions, open graph tags, and cross-language links. Doing it by hand meant nothing was auto-generated or mismatched, but it also meant 16 opportunities to get something wrong. Consistency required a checklist approach for every new page.

Trust without heaviness

Legal services demand seriousness, but a site that feels stiff or overly formal loses people before they read a word. The navy and gold palette, the Kaisei Decol serif in the mission statement, and the warm cream backgrounds were all chosen to feel credible and human at the same time.
